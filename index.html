HyTech Racing

Formula SAE Electric Competition Team

Role: Electrical Lead
Results: 3rd overall, 3rd in acceleration, 3rd in skidpad, 4th in design, and 2nd in cost
Dates: June 2021 to July 2022

Synopsis

HyTech Racing is Georgia Tech's Formula SAE Electric competition team. The annual competition challenges teams to design and build a fully electric single-seat race car and race against more than fifty other teams. As electrical lead for HT06, the car for the 2021-2022 competition year, I led the electrical team in the overall design and validation of the electrical systems of the vehicle.

Battery Management System (BMS)
Source code: Driver Backend Library

I architected the clean-sheet redesign of the BMS embedded software. The software has several main functions:

  • Issue commands to the battery stack monitor chips
  • Read and buffer data from the battery stack monitor chips
  • Process data and perform safety checks
  • Immediately disconnect the high voltage battery from the motor in the event of a safety violation
  • Balance the battery cells when charging
  • Send data to data logging unit via CAN bus

Issues with Old Program Solutions in New Program
Relied on outdated manufacturer code which was difficult to extend and maintain. Use custom object-oriented paradigm to represent and control each individual battery stack monitor.
Timed battery monitor communications with waits up to two seconds long, effectively freezing the microcontroller for that duration. Times battery communications with a state machine within the control loop, eliminating waits altogether.
No mechanism to detect microcontroller freezes. Interrupt to toggle watchdog timer that disconnects high voltage battery if microcontroller freezes.
Used third-party SPI communication library, which could not guarantee compatiblity with all Arduino-ecosystem devices. Uses standard Arduino SPI communication library. Compatible with all Arduino-ecosystem devices.

Circuit Design and Validation
Hardware Designs: Board Files (AutoDesk EAGLE) Design Binder (PDF)

The team designs and validates all circuit boards in house, barring a few that competition rules require be purchased from vendors. During my year as lead, we introduced several new circuit boards and revised all the rest.

I assisted the electrical subteam leads in the design of the boards they were responsible for. I played a primary role in the validation of the accumulator management unit (ACU) and tractive system board (TSB), the two boards that control and monitor the battery. Our improvements enabled our maximum pack voltage to increase from 300V to 350V, enhanced driver awareness of vehicle state, and increased the collection of data.

ChatPCB

ImmerseGT XR Hackathon

Role: Team Member
Results: Winner, Next-Gen XR Apps - Best Commercial Use
Dates: April 2023

Synopsis

ChatPCB was my team's submission to the 2023 ImmerseGT Hackathon. The hackathon challenges participants to build novel virtual and augmented reality software within a 36-hour time limit.

Motivation

To hand-solder parts onto a printed circuit board (PCB), one must look up the part number in a table or database, find the part by part number from their parts bin, and find the location for the part on the board by searching for the reference designator in the CAD program.

The process is tedious and requires several context switches, resulting in high work times, fatigue, and mistakes.

Our Application
Software: Github Repository

We developed an Android application that tracks a PCB through the camera viewfinder. The app listens for the user to read off a reference designator on the PCB. It marks the location of the spoken designator with a virtual marker and displays the relevant part number and type.

Benefits:

  • Find the part location and part number in one software with one spoken command.
  • Visual marker remembers part location for you as you search parts bin. No need to search for location after part is found.
  • Visual marker is tolerant to movement: as long as the PCB is in frame, the marker moves along with the PCB.
  • Integrates seamlessly with AutoDesk EAGLE CAD software.

PCB Tracking: The app utilizes ARCore Augmented Images to track the PCB. A reference image of the PCB is required, which is obtained with the reference image AutoDesk EAGLE generates automatically.

Parts Information: Parts information is obtained by parsing the bill of materials generated by AutoDesk EAGLE.

Voice Recognition: Voice recognition of reference designators is implemented using the SpeechRecognizer API.
